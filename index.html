Notes from the first lesson.

The Internet

...is a bunch of computers "talking" to each other!

The World Wide Web

...is a large collections of interlinked files. The majority of the content is contained within HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) documents.

You sit at your computer interfacing with the internet, primarily through your browser. Servers hold the information, and the info is transmitted via a protocol called HTTP.

HTML

...documents are made up of the content (the what of it all) and the markup (how the "what" looks)! (Ok, ok...Let's stop sounding like the caterpillar from Alice in Wonderland.)

Some of the inline tags learned in this lesson were:
-Span
- Bold
- em
And, of course, the br tag, which has helped us format this list, even without using an ul! (We also learned the img tag, but let's not insert pics here yet.)

We also learned a couple block tags, including: "div" and "p". (The block tags have an "invisible box" around them.)

We took a look at the general layout of an element, which included the tag itself, the attribute(s) with their values, and for tags other than the void ones, the content within.

Computers are "Stupid"

They just follow our instructions, even though some of those instructions might be fairly complex! (Until the robot uprising, that is! LOL.) This gives us the opportunity to wield a lot of control with our instructions. But it also means that we have to issue those instructions very accurately.
